Xinity AI is a self-hosted AI platform for enterprises that cannot send data to the cloud — not because of preference, but because of law, regulation, or trade secrets. It gives you model orchestration, an OpenAI-compatible API, a management dashboard, fine-tuning pipelines, and multi-node GPU scaling, all running entirely on your own hardware.

## Why Xinity exists

Cloud AI assumes your data can leave the building. For many enterprises it can't, not without violating GDPR, banking secrecy laws, journalistic source protection, attorney-client privilege, or trade secret law. These organizations aren't cloud-skeptical — they're **sovereignty-blocked**.

Xinity solves this by bringing the full AI operations layer on-premises: nothing leaves your infrastructure, not to a region, not to a cloud, not at all.

### The Utilization Inversion

Cloud AI pricing is built around bursty, ~15% average utilization. Always-on AI agents and internal tooling run at 80–90%. At that utilization level, dedicated on-premises infrastructure delivers roughly **80% cost savings** compared to equivalent cloud capacity. Sovereignty isn't just a compliance requirement — it's an economic advantage. Xinity calls this phenomenon the **Utilization Inversion**.

## How Xinity compares

Ollama and vLLM are excellent inference engines — Xinity uses them under the hood. Xinity adds the full enterprise operations layer on top: orchestration, access control, observability, and governance.
